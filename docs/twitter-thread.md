Suggested tags: `@BagsApp`, `@finnbags`, `@hunterjisaacson`, `@BagsHackathon`

## Thread

1. nuke.fm turns trader bets into public price forecasts for Bags project shares.

Bags tokens already have spot charts. nuke.fm adds a forward price: where traders think each project share will trade by expiry.

2. Memecoin traders have plenty of current-state data: price, volume, market cap, holder movement, and timeline flow.

The missing piece is a public forecast that turns conviction and doubt into prices.

3. Each Bags token gets a long-term scalar market.

Traders buy LONG if they think the token will trade higher by expiry, or SHORT if they think it will trade lower.

4. The market price becomes the forecast.

nuke.fm converts the live LONG price into an implied expiry price, then shows the implied move next to prediction liquidity, prediction volume, token volume, and token market cap.

5. SHORT exposure matters.

Without it, bearish traders sell spot or stay quiet. With it, downside views become visible and help make the public forecast sharper.

6. This is not a 5m market.

Five-minute markets price the next burst of order flow. nuke.fm is built for project-share questions: creator execution, liquidity, holder quality, and narrative durability.

7. The predicted price should not always equal spot.

Spot is the current clearing price. nuke.fm's predicted price is the market's forward price for expiry.

8. Bots can trade too.

The nuke.fm bot reads the public board, gathers web context, asks an LLM for a cited expiry-price forecast, posts its rationale, quotes the market, and trades inside risk caps.

9. Prediction rationales matter because they make the thesis inspectable.

You can compare the forecast, sources, confidence, and position value instead of only seeing that a trade happened.

10. For Bags traders, the board is a faster way to see where conviction is forming, where downside is being priced, and which project shares the market thinks are mispriced.
